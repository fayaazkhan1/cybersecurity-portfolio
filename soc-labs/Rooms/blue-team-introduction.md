# Blue Team Introduction

**Path**: Junior SOC Analyst / Blue Team Fundamentals  
**Date Completed**: 2026-07-23  
**Category**: SOC, Blue Team, Defensive Security

## Overview
This set of rooms provided a solid introduction to Blue Team operations. I explored the role of SOC Analysts, how humans and systems are targeted as attack vectors, and how defenders can effectively monitor, investigate, and respond to threats.

## Rooms Covered
1. **Junior SOC Analyst** – Alert investigation and response
2. **SOC Role in Blue Team** – SOC structure and responsibilities
3. **Humans as Attack Vectors** – Social Engineering awareness
4. **Systems as Attack Vectors** – System misconfigurations and hardening

## Key Takeaways
- SOC workflow: monitoring SIEM alerts → investigation → escalation → containment
- How to use tools like IP reputation checkers and firewalls to respond to threats
- Humans are often the weakest link (phishing, pretexting, etc.)
- Systems can be exploited through misconfigurations — proper hardening is essential
- Using security alerts to drive proactive policy improvements
- Importance of both reactive (incident response) and proactive (awareness & hardening) defenses

## High-Level Skills Practiced
- Reading and prioritizing SIEM alerts
- Validating malicious IPs and blocking them
- Understanding Blue Team roles and escalation procedures
- Recognizing social engineering techniques
- Translating alerts into better security policies

## Reflection
These four rooms gave me a well-rounded introduction to Blue Team fundamentals. I particularly enjoyed seeing the full cycle: detecting threats in the SIEM, investigating them, responding (e.g., firewall blocks), and learning how to prevent future incidents by addressing both human and system vulnerabilities.

The most valuable insight was understanding how alerts should inform security policy decisions. It connected theory with practice and showed how Blue Team work complements offensive security knowledge.

**Biggest takeaway**: Effective defense requires awareness of both technical vulnerabilities and human factors, combined with fast response capabilities.

**Overall Difficulty**: Easy to Medium  
**Recommendation**: Highly recommended as a starting point for anyone interested in SOC or Blue Team roles.
